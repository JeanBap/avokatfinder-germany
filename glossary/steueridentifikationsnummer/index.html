Steueridentifikationsnummer (Tax ID)

The German 11-digit tax identification number (Steuer-ID) assigned to every person registered in Germany, required for all tax filings and financial transactions.

Home › Legal Glossary › Steueridentifikationsnummer (Tax ID)
TL;DR The German 11-digit tax identification number (Steuer-ID) assigned to every person registered in Germany, required for all tax filings and financial transactions.

The Steueridentifikationsnummer (Steuer-ID or IdNr — Identifikationsnummer) is an 11-digit personal tax identification number assigned to every person registered as a resident in Germany. It is issued automatically by the Bundeszentralamt für Steuern (BZSt — Federal Central Tax Office) after Anmeldung (registration) and is used on all tax returns, employment contracts, investment income filings, and property-related tax matters.

The Steuer-ID is separate from the Steuernummer (tax number) assigned by the local Finanzamt (tax office) when you file your first tax return. The Steuer-ID is a permanent lifetime identifier — it remains the same even if you move Finanzamt. The Steuernummer changes when you move between Finanzamt districts. Both may be required for different purposes.

For property transactions, the Finanzamt requires the buyer's Steuer-ID when calculating and collecting Grunderwerbsteuer (transfer tax). Landlords must include their Steuer-ID on rent receipts and invoices. If you receive German rental income as a non-resident, you may be subject to German income tax and must register with the Finanzamt to obtain a Steuernummer for filing purposes.

Non-resident property buyers who do not register as German residents do not automatically receive a Steuer-ID. They must apply for a Steuernummer directly from the competent Finanzamt for the area where the property is located. This Steuernummer is then used for German tax filings related to the property (rental income, capital gains on sale).

In Germany, all employers, investment providers, and banks must withhold taxes at source (Quellensteuer) and remit them using the employee's or customer's Steuer-ID. Keeping your Steuer-ID current and accessible is essential for smooth financial administration in Germany.

Key Facts

Format11-digit permanent number — different from Steuernummer
Issued byBundeszentralamt für Steuern (BZSt)
TriggersAutomatic on Anmeldung — or direct application for non-residents
PermanentDoes not change with address or Finanzamt changes
Non-residentsMust apply directly to relevant Finanzamt for Steuernummer
⚠ Common Mistake: Confusing the Steuer-ID (permanent national ID) with the Steuernummer (local Finanzamt number). Giving the wrong one to an employer or bank can cause tax withholding errors that take months to correct.
💡 Expert Tip: If you have lost your Steuer-ID, request a new notification letter from the BZSt website (idnr.de) — processing takes about four weeks. Do not try to apply for a new one; you already have one and cannot get a second.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a Steuer-ID to open a German bank account?

Yes. German banks are required by law (§154 AO) to collect and report the Steuer-ID of account holders to the Bundeszentralamt. Without a Steuer-ID, most German banks will not open an account. This makes obtaining the Anmeldung and resulting Steuer-ID a priority for newly arrived expats.

How do I get my Steuer-ID as a newly registered resident?

After completing your Anmeldung at the Einwohnermeldeamt (residents' registration office), the Bundeszentralamt automatically sends your Steuer-ID to your registered address within two to four weeks. If you need it sooner, contact your local Finanzamt — they may be able to provide it after verifying your registration.

Is the Steuer-ID the same as the European TIN?

For German tax residents, the Steuer-ID serves as Germany's Tax Identification Number (TIN) in the EU context. It is the number required on international financial account reporting forms (CRS/FATCA) and on European cross-border employment and pension declarations.
