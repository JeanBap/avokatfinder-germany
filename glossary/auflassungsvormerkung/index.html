Auflassungsvormerkung (Priority Notice)

The Auflassungsvormerkung is a German land registry notation that reserves the buyer's right to receive title to a property, protecting against the seller selling to someone else or creating new encumbrances.

TL;DR The Auflassungsvormerkung is a German land registry notation that reserves the buyer's right to receive title to a property, protecting against the seller selling to someone else or creating new encumbrances.

The Auflassungsvormerkung (priority notice or reservation notice) is a crucial protection for property buyers in Germany. Registered in the Grundbuch (land registry) immediately after the notarial signing of the Kaufvertrag, it creates a public reservation of the buyer's right to receive title. Any subsequent encumbrances or transfers by the seller registered after the Auflassungsvormerkung have no effect against the buyer.

Without the Auflassungsvormerkung, the buyer would be vulnerable in the period between signing the contract and receiving final registered title — a period that can last eight to twelve weeks while purchase price payment, tax clearance, and land registry formalities are completed. During this window, the seller could theoretically mortgage the property or sell it to a third party.

The Auflassungsvormerkung is registered by the notary as one of the first actions after the Kaufvertrag is signed, typically within a few days. The notary submits the registration request to the Grundbuchamt (land registry). Once registered, the buyer's interest is protected — any subsequent liens, judgments, or insolvency proceedings against the seller cannot affect the buyer's right to receive clear title.

The Auflassungsvormerkung appears in Section II of the Grundbuch (the "Lasten und Beschränkungen" — charges and restrictions section). It shows the buyer's name and a reference to the purchase contract date. When the final Auflassung (title transfer) is completed, the Vormerkung is cancelled and replaced by the buyer's ownership entry in Section I.

German mortgage banks require the Auflassungsvormerkung to be registered before releasing purchase funds. This ensures the bank's security interest (Grundschuld) will rank ahead of any other claims registered after the Vormerkung date. The interplay between Auflassungsvormerkung and Grundschuld registration timing is something the notary coordinates carefully.

⚠ Common Mistake: Releasing purchase funds before the Auflassungsvormerkung is confirmed as registered. The notary should confirm registration before requesting payment. Paying without the Vormerkung leaves the buyer exposed if the seller becomes insolvent before the transfer is complete.
💡 Expert Tip: Request a copy of the Grundbuchauszug (land registry extract) from the notary showing the registered Auflassungsvormerkung before paying the purchase price. This is standard practice and any reputable notary will provide it automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the Auflassungsvormerkung cannot be registered because of existing encumbrances?

The notary checks the Grundbuch before the signing appointment. If unexpected encumbrances are discovered, the notary will flag them. The Kaufvertrag can include a condition requiring a clean Grundbuch before the purchase price is paid. Do not pay before all blocking entries are resolved.

Does the Auflassungsvormerkung protect me against the seller's bankruptcy?

Yes. If the seller becomes insolvent after the Auflassungsvormerkung is registered, the insolvency administrator cannot include the property in the estate as against the buyer. The buyer's right to receive title is protected by the priority established by the Vormerkung date. This is one of its most important functions.

Is the Auflassungsvormerkung the same as the Auflassung?

No. The Auflassungsvormerkung is a preliminary notation protecting the buyer's future right to receive title. The Auflassung is the formal transfer of ownership itself — the mutual declaration by seller and buyer to the notary agreeing to the transfer. The Auflassung is registered after the purchase price is paid and tax clearance is received.
