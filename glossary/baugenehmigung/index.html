Baugenehmigung (Building Permit)

A German Baugenehmigung is the official local authority permit required before constructing, significantly extending, or altering buildings in Germany.

Home › Legal Glossary › Baugenehmigung (Building Permit)
TL;DR A German Baugenehmigung is the official local authority permit required before constructing, significantly extending, or altering buildings in Germany.

The Baugenehmigung (building permit) is the formal approval from the Bauordnungsamt (building authority) of the local Kreisfreie Stadt or Landkreis that is required before most construction, extension, or structural alteration of buildings in Germany. Building without a permit (Schwarzbau — black build) is an administrative offence and can result in demolition orders and significant fines.

Applications for a Baugenehmigung are submitted by a qualified Entwurfsverfasser (usually a licensed architect) on behalf of the building owner. Required documents typically include: site plan, floor plans, sections, elevations, structural calculations, and compliance documentation with applicable building codes (Landesbauordnung — varies by state). Processing takes two to six months depending on complexity and local authority.

Germany's building regulations are governed at state (Land) level — each of the 16 Länder has its own Landesbauordnung (state building code). While the overall system is similar, specific requirements for window sizes, room heights, parking spaces, and energy efficiency vary between states. Buyers of development property must check the applicable state code carefully.

When purchasing a property with existing structures, always verify that planning permission (Baugenehmigung) was obtained for all buildings and extensions visible on the property. Many properties in Germany carry undeclared Schwarzbauten (unpermitted structures) — outbuildings, conservatories, loft conversions — which can cause problems for insurance, financing, and future sale.

Some minor works are genehmigungsfrei (permit-free) under the applicable Landesbauordnung. Examples vary by state but typically include minor outbuildings under a certain floor area, garden fences, and internal alterations that do not affect the structure or fire safety. Even permit-free works must comply with the building code — they are simply exempt from advance approval.

⚠ Common Mistake: Buying a property with an extension or outbuilding without checking whether it has a Baugenehmigung. Schwarzbauten can invalidate building insurance, trigger demolition orders, and complicate future sale or mortgage applications.
💡 Expert Tip: Request the Baugenehmigungsunterlagen (permit documents) from the current owner and cross-reference them with the actual state of the property. Any discrepancies should be investigated and resolved before exchange of contracts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I apply for a Baugenehmigung retroactively for an existing unpermitted structure?

Some states allow retroactive approval (Baugenehmigung im Nachhinein) if the structure complies with current building regulations. However, approval is not guaranteed — if the structure violates setback rules, building lines, or other regulations, demolition may be required. Always seek specialist planning law advice before purchasing property with unpermitted structures.

How long is a Baugenehmigung valid in Germany?

Most Baugenehmigungen are valid for three to four years from the date of issue (the exact period varies by state). If construction has not begun within the valid period, the permit lapses and a new application must be submitted. Extensions (Verlängerung) are generally available before the expiry date.

Is a Baugenehmigung the same as a building regulations approval?

In Germany, the Baugenehmigung combines both planning approval (whether the development is permitted) and structural safety approval (how it must be built). The Landesbauordnung governs both aspects in one document, unlike the UK system where they are separate. Some aspects (like fire safety) may still require separate specialist certifications.
