Erbschaftsteuer (Inheritance Tax)

German inheritance tax — levied on assets received by inheritance or gift, with rates of 7–50% and exemptions depending on the relationship between donor and recipient.

Home › Legal Glossary › Erbschaftsteuer (Inheritance Tax)
TL;DR German inheritance tax — levied on assets received by inheritance or gift, with rates of 7–50% and exemptions depending on the relationship between donor and recipient.

Erbschaftsteuer (inheritance tax) and Schenkungsteuer (gift tax) are companion taxes under the Erbschaft- und Schenkungsteuergesetz (ErbStG) that apply to assets transferred on death or by gift in Germany. The tax applies to German residents and to non-residents who inherit or receive German-situated assets (including real estate). Rates range from 7% to 50% depending on the relationship between donor and recipient and the value of the inheritance.

German inheritance tax is structured around three tax classes (Steuerklassen): Class I (spouses/partners, children, grandchildren, parents) enjoys the most favourable rates (7–30%) and highest personal exemptions; Class II (siblings, nieces/nephews, parents-in-law, step-children) faces rates of 15–43%; Class III (all others, including unmarried partners) faces rates of 30–50%.

Personal exemptions (Freibeträge) are generous for close family: €500,000 for spouses/registered partners, €400,000 for children (per child, per parent — applicable every 10 years), €200,000 for grandchildren. These exemptions apply every 10 years and can be used for gifts as well as inheritance. Long-term planning using gift allowances can significantly reduce the eventual inheritance tax burden.

German real estate is subject to Erbschaftsteuer based on the Grundbesitzwert (standard assessed value) of the property, not necessarily the market value. The assessed value can be lower than the market value, which sometimes reduces the tax liability. However, recent court decisions have narrowed the gap between assessed and market values.

Inheritance tax returns (Erbschaftsteuererklärung) must be filed with the Finanzamt within three months of the inheritance becoming known. Failure to file is a tax offence. Inheritance of German real estate by non-resident foreign nationals is subject to German inheritance tax even if the recipient lives outside Germany. EU residents benefit from most German exemptions; non-EU residents may face a higher basic allowance threshold.

💡 Expert Tip: If you inherit German real estate as a non-resident, immediately consult a German Steuerberater (tax adviser) with Erbschaftsteuer expertise. The filing deadlines are strict and the cross-border interaction with inheritance taxes in your home country requires specialist advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a surviving spouse automatically exempt from German inheritance tax?

No, but they have a very high exemption (€500,000) and access to a pension-equivalent allowance (Versorgungsfreibetrag of up to €256,000 for spouses). For large estates exceeding these thresholds, tax will be due. German registered partners (Lebenspartner) have the same Class I treatment as spouses since 2009.

What is the Güterstand and how does it affect inheritance?

The Güterstand (matrimonial property regime) affects the share of an estate a surviving spouse receives. The default German Güterstand is Zugewinngemeinschaft (accrued gains community). On the death of one spouse, the survivor receives both their inheritance share and an additional Zugewinnausgleich (accrued gains equalization). This can significantly reduce the taxable inheritance.

Does Germany have a double taxation treaty for inheritance tax with the UK?

Germany has inheritance tax treaties with a limited number of countries. Germany and the UK do not have a bilateral inheritance tax treaty. Where both countries tax the same inheritance, relief may be available under unilateral foreign tax credit provisions in each country's domestic law. Specialist cross-border estate planning advice is essential.
