Energieausweis (Energy Performance Certificate)

Germany's mandatory energy performance certificate for residential and commercial buildings — required for all property sales and most lettings, rating energy efficiency on a scale.

TL;DR Germany's mandatory energy performance certificate for residential and commercial buildings — required for all property sales and most lettings, rating energy efficiency on a scale.

The Energieausweis (energy performance certificate) is a document certifying the energy efficiency of a German building, required under the Gebäudeenergiegesetz (GEG — Building Energy Act). It is mandatory for nearly all residential and commercial properties sold or let in Germany. The certificate provides a standardised assessment of the building's energy demand or consumption and recommends improvement measures.

There are two types of Energieausweis: the Bedarfsausweis (demand certificate), which is based on a technical assessment of the building's insulation, heating system, and structural characteristics, and the Verbrauchsausweis (consumption certificate), which is based on actual energy consumption data from the last three years. The Bedarfsausweis is generally considered more reliable but costs more to produce.

The energy efficiency class runs from A+ (most efficient) to H (least efficient), with colour coding from green to red. New buildings since 2020 must meet Effizienzhaus 55 standard (approximately B or better). Older stock is frequently rated D, E, or F. From 2024, properties rated F and G face increasing restrictions on their marketability under EU energy efficiency directives.

When selling or letting a property, the Energieausweis must be presented to prospective buyers or tenants at the first viewing. The energy class and specific consumption figures must also be included in property listings (Exposé). Failing to provide the Energieausweis can result in fines of up to €15,000 from the Bundesamt für Wirtschaft und Ausfuhrkontrolle (BAFA).

The Energieausweis is valid for ten years from the date of issue. For buyers, the certificate is a useful indication of energy costs and potential retrofit investment — a poorly rated property may require expensive insulation or heating system upgrades under coming regulations. Always factor the cost of future energy improvements into the purchase price negotiation.

PenaltyUp to €15,000 fine for non-provision
⚠ Common Mistake: Accepting a Verbrauchsausweis without asking to see consumption data for the actual property. Average consumption figures can mask poor building fabric if the previous tenant was away frequently or kept the heating very low.
💡 Expert Tip: Use the Energieausweis as a negotiating tool. A G or H rated property may require €20,000–50,000 of investment in insulation and a heat pump to meet future EU minimum standards. Quantify the retrofit cost before making an offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Energieausweis required for all buildings?

Most buildings require one, but there are exceptions: buildings under 50 sqm, listed buildings where compliance would alter their character, churches, and some temporary structures. For mixed-use buildings, different sections may have different requirements. Check with a specialist if your property falls into an unusual category.

How much does an Energieausweis cost?

A Verbrauchsausweis (consumption-based) costs approximately €25–100 if ordered online from an accredited issuer with proper documentation. A Bedarfsausweis (demand-based, requiring an on-site inspection) costs €200–500 depending on building size and complexity.

Can I negotiate a lower price because of a poor energy rating?

Yes, and this is increasingly common in Germany. Properties rated F, G, or H carry implicit future upgrade costs under EU energy transition regulations. A realistic assessment of the renovation cost required to reach at least D or C provides a factual basis for a price reduction.
