Legal Services for Foreign Nationals in Nuremberg

Nuremberg (Bavaria) has approximately 520,000 residents with foreign nationals representing around 24%. Known as Bavaria's second city, toy and electronics industries, historic old town, the city attracts international workers, students, and families drawn by its electronics, automation, healthcare, and the Nuremberg trade fair.

Foreign nationals living in Germany need legal advice on residence permits (Aufenthaltserlaubnis), work authorisation, property law, employment rights, and family law. German lawyers (Rechtsanwaelte) are regulated by their regional Bar Association (Rechtsanwaltskammer) and must hold professional indemnity insurance.

Avokatfinder lists verified English-speaking lawyers in Nuremberg with confirmed expertise in immigration, employment, property, and family law matters for international residents.

Frequently Asked Questions: Lawyers in Nuremberg

What immigration services are in Nuremberg?

Nuremberg has a large Turkish, Eastern European, and Asian community alongside international workers in its technology and manufacturing sectors. Immigration lawyers handle EU Blue Card applications, family reunification visas, Niederlassungserlaubnis, and German citizenship applications. The Nuremberg foreigners office (Auslaenderbehorde) is one of Bavaria's busiest.

What is the Grunderwerbsteuer (property transfer tax) in Bavaria?

Bavaria has one of Germany's lowest property transfer tax rates at 3.5% of the purchase price. Combined with notary fees (1-1.5%), land registry fees (0.5%), and estate agent fees (3.57% if applicable), total additional costs on a Nuremberg property run approximately 6-8%. Your lawyer reviews the Kaufvertrag and coordinates notarisation.

Can foreign nationals buy property in Nuremberg without restrictions?

Yes. Germany places no nationality restrictions on property ownership. EU and non-EU citizens alike can purchase residential or commercial property freely. Your lawyer reviews the purchase agreement, checks for any encumbrances (Grundschulden, Dienstbarkeiten) in the land register (Grundbuch), and coordinates completion at the notary.

What is the path to German citizenship through naturalisation in Nuremberg?

Germany reformed naturalisation rules in 2024. The standard path requires 5 years of lawful residence (down from 8), German language B1 level, financial self-sufficiency, and no criminal convictions. Special contributions can shorten this to 3 years. A Nuremberg immigration lawyer checks your eligibility and advises on the application to the Nuremberg naturalisation office.

Are there English-speaking lawyers in Nuremberg?

Yes. Nuremberg's international business community and university sectors support a number of English-speaking law firms. Avokatfinder lists lawyers with verified English language competency alongside their practice areas. Several Nuremberg firms also speak Turkish, Russian, Italian, and other languages reflecting the city's diverse community.
