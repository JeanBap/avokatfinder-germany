How to Choose a Lawyer in Germany

Rechtsanwalt, Notar, Fachanwalt, Steuerberater — find the right professional for your situation

TL;DR

Germany has distinct legal professionals: Rechtsanwalt (general lawyer), Fachanwalt (specialist), Notar (notary — mandatory for property/GmbH), Steuerberater (tax adviser). Courts above Amtsgericht require a Rechtsanwalt. Fees are governed by the RVG for statutory work; hourly rates EUR 200–500/hr in major cities.

✅ You need: Notar (Notary)

Property purchases in Germany require a Notar — this is mandatory under BGB § 311b. The Notar is neutral and cannot represent either party.

🔑 What the Notar does: Drafts the Kaufvertrag, checks land register (Grundbuch), coordinates Grunderwerbsteuer payment, and registers ownership. Both buyer and seller attend signing.
💶 Notar fees: Fixed by GNotKG based on purchase price. For EUR 400,000 property: approx. EUR 1,600–2,000 (Notar) + EUR 400–600 (Grundbuchamt). Plus Grunderwerbsteuer 3.5%–6.5% (varies by Bundesland).
⚠️ Note: If a legal dispute arises (e.g. seller misrepresented condition), you'll also need a Rechtsanwalt — Notare don't litigate.

✅ You need: Notar + Rechtsanwalt (Fachanwalt für Miet- und Wohnungseigentumsrecht)

Commercial or disputed property transactions require both notarial authentication and specialist legal advice.

🏢 For commercial leases, development projects, or property disputes: a Fachanwalt für Miet- und WEG-Recht will protect your interests in negotiations and proceedings.
💶 Typical cost: Notar fees (GNotKG scale) + Rechtsanwalt EUR 300–500/hr or RVG scale. For EUR 2M commercial deal: Notar ~EUR 8,000–12,000; RA negotiation fees ~EUR 5,000–15,000.

✅ You need: Notar (+ optional Rechtsanwalt for complex structure)

GmbH and UG formation requires a notarised Gesellschaftsvertrag (articles of association) under GmbHG § 2. UG can use the Musterprotokoll (standard template) — cheaper and faster.

🏢 UG (haftungsbeschränkt): EUR 1 minimum capital. Musterprotokoll: Notar fee ~EUR 100–200. Must retain 25% of annual profit until EUR 25,000 capital reached.
🏢 GmbH: EUR 25,000 minimum capital (EUR 12,500 paid in). Custom Satzung: Notar fee EUR 500–2,000 depending on complexity. Handelsregister fee ~EUR 150.
💡 For complex shareholder structures, IP rights, or investor terms, add a Fachanwalt für Gesellschaftsrecht to draft the Shareholder Agreement (Gesellschaftervereinbarung).

✅ You need: Fachanwalt für Gesellschaftsrecht + Wirtschaftsprüfer

AG formations and complex restructurings require specialist corporate law advice and auditing.

💶 AG minimum capital: EUR 50,000. AG formation involves Gründungsbericht (founding report) audited by Wirtschaftsprüfer. Legal fees: EUR 10,000–50,000+ depending on complexity.

✅ You need: Rechtsanwalt (no Notar required for GbR/OHG)

Partnerships (GbR, OHG, KG) do not require notarisation, but a written partnership agreement is strongly recommended.

📝 A Rechtsanwalt can draft a Gesellschaftsvertrag for EUR 500–2,000 depending on complexity. Without a written agreement, BGB § 705 default rules apply — often unfavourable.

⚠️ URGENT: Fachanwalt für Arbeitsrecht — 3-WEEK DEADLINE

You have 21 days from receipt of the dismissal letter to file a Kündigungsschutzklage (KSchG § 4). After this, the dismissal is deemed valid regardless of legality.

🚨 Do this today: Call a Fachanwalt für Arbeitsrecht or your Gewerkschaft (trade union). Many offer same-day appointments for dismissal matters.
💶 Legal aid: If you cannot afford a lawyer, apply for Beratungshilfe (legal advice) at your local Amtsgericht — costs only EUR 15.
📊 RVG fees: For Streitwert = 3 months' gross salary. E.g. EUR 4,500/month → Streitwert EUR 13,500 → 1.3 Verfahrensgebühr ~EUR 886 (excl. VAT). Many firms offer initial consultation ~EUR 190.

✅ You need: Fachanwalt für Arbeitsrecht

Employment contracts, discrimination, wage claims, maternity rights, and workplace disputes require specialist employment law advice.

💶 Initial consultation: EUR 150–250. Many firms offer flat-fee services for contract reviews (EUR 150–400 for standard employment contract review).
🏢 Rechtsschutzversicherung tip: Employment legal insurance costs ~EUR 5–15/month and covers most Arbeitsrecht disputes. Worth getting before you need it.

✅ You need: Fachanwalt für Steuerrecht

Tax disputes with the Finanzamt, Einspruch (objection) proceedings, and Finanzgericht (FG) cases require a Fachanwalt für Steuerrecht or a Steuerberater with litigation experience.

📋 Process: (1) File Einspruch within 1 month of tax assessment (§ 355 AO); (2) if rejected → appeal to Finanzgericht; (3) final appeal to Bundesfinanzhof (BFH) — Anwaltszwang applies at BFH level.
💶 FG proceedings: court fees based on disputed amount + lawyer fees (RVG or hourly). For EUR 30,000 disputed tax: total cost risk ~EUR 8,000–15,000.

✅ You need: Steuerberater (Tax Adviser)

Tax returns, VAT, payroll, and annual accounts are handled by Steuerberater — they are not lawyers but are licensed for all tax advisory work.

💶 Fees: Governed by StBVV (Steuerberatervergütungsverordnung) for statutory services. Typical: EUR 80–200/hr. Annual accounts for a small GmbH: EUR 1,500–4,000.
🔗 Note: For tax disputes that escalate to FG/BFH, switch to a Fachanwalt für Steuerrecht — Steuerberater can represent at FG but not BGH level.

✅ You need: Fachanwalt für Ausländer- und Asylrecht

Visa refusals, deportation orders, and asylum proceedings are high-stakes — specialist advice is essential. Deadlines are often 2–4 weeks.

⏰ Deportation notice: File Antrag auf aufschiebende Wirkung immediately to suspend removal pending appeal (VwGO § 80). Same-day action may be required.
🏢 Free advice: Caritas, AWO, and Diakonie provide free immigration advice in most cities. Beratungshilfe available at Amtsgericht (EUR 15).

✅ You need: Rechtsanwalt (Immigration Specialist)

EU Blue Card, Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz skilled worker visas, and Niederlassungserlaubnis applications benefit from specialist immigration lawyers, though most straightforward cases can be handled without one.

📋 2025 thresholds: EU Blue Card: EUR 48,300 general / EUR 37,590 shortage occupations (STEM, healthcare, IT). Chancenkarte (opportunity card): 6 points needed.
💶 Lawyer fees for standard Blue Card application: EUR 500–1,500. Complex cases (employer + employee joint representation): EUR 2,000–5,000.

✅ You can self-represent at Amtsgericht (optional: Rechtsanwalt)

For claims under EUR 5,000, you may represent yourself at the Amtsgericht. However, a brief consultation (1 hour) with a Rechtsanwalt can significantly improve your position.

💶 Initial consultation: Many German lawyers offer a 1-hour consultation for EUR 150–250. The Erstberatungsgebühr is capped at EUR 190 incl. VAT under RVG § 34.
🏢 Schlichtung first: Consider a Schlichtungsstelle (arbitration board) — free or low-cost and faster than court. The Universalschlichtungsstelle des Bundes handles most B2C disputes.

✅ You need: Rechtsanwalt (Anwaltszwang applies)

At Landgericht level (claims EUR 5,000+), you must be represented by a Rechtsanwalt. Both parties bear their own lawyers' costs if they win; loser pays all.

💶 For Streitwert EUR 50,000: statutory Verfahrensgebühr 1.3 = ~EUR 1,785 per side + court fees ~EUR 1,638. Total risk if you lose: ~EUR 7,000–10,000.
💡 Consider mediation (Mediation) first — courts actively encourage this under § 278a ZPO. Mediator fees: EUR 1,500–5,000 shared, much cheaper than litigation.

✅ You need: Specialist Rechtsanwalt with OLG/BGH admission

Appeals at OLG and BGH require lawyers with specific experience and, at BGH level, special BGH Zulassung (only ~50 Rechtsanwälte are admitted to the BGH).

💶 BGH cases involve significant costs — total legal fees often EUR 30,000–200,000+ depending on complexity and duration.

✅ You need: Fachanwalt für Familienrecht

Divorce, custody, and maintenance matters are handled in the Familiengericht (family court division of Amtsgericht). Anwaltszwang applies for divorce proceedings.

📋 Scheidung (divorce): Minimum 1-year Trennungszeit (separation period) required (§ 1565 BGB). Joint petition possible with one shared Anwalt — cheaper.
💶 For divorce with pension equalisation (Versorgungsausgleich) and no contested assets: total cost EUR 2,000–5,000. Contested divorce with asset dispute: EUR 10,000–50,000+.

✅ You need: Notar (for drafting) + optional Rechtsanwalt (for advice)

Wills (Testamente), Erbverträge (inheritance contracts), and formal inheritance waivers require notarisation under BGB §§ 2231, 2276, 2348.

💶 Notar fees for a simple will: EUR 100–500. Erbvertrag with complex asset distribution: EUR 500–3,000+ depending on net estate value.
💡 A holographic (handwritten) will (eigenhändiges Testament) under BGB § 2247 does NOT require a Notar, but a Notar-drafted will is much harder to contest.

✅ You need: Fachanwalt für Erbrecht

Inheritance disputes, Pflichtteil (forced share) claims, and Erbausschlagung (inheritance disclaimer) require specialist estate law advice.

📋 Pflichtteil: Disinherited spouses and children are entitled to half their statutory share (BGB § 2303). Claim must be raised within 3 years (BGB § 2332).
💶 Fachanwalt für Erbrecht fees: RVG based on estate value. For EUR 200,000 estate: Verfahrensgebühr 1.3 ~EUR 2,350. Initial consultation ~EUR 190–250.

✅ You need: Rechtsanwalt (Fachanwalt für IT-/Vertragsrecht for complex)

Contract drafting and review is a core Rechtsanwalt service. For IT contracts, IP licensing, or SaaS agreements, a Fachanwalt für IT-Recht is preferable.

💶 Standard commercial contract review: EUR 200–600 flat fee. Complex technology or IP agreement: EUR 1,000–5,000. NDA drafting: EUR 150–400.

✅ You need: Rechtsanwalt — respond to Abmahnung within deadline

An Abmahnung (cease and desist) typically demands response within 7–14 days. Ignoring it leads to injunction proceedings (einstweilige Verfügung) — much more expensive.

⚠️ Check the deadline carefully. If it's shorter than 7 days or received late, call a Rechtsanwalt immediately.
💶 Response to standard Abmahnung: EUR 300–800 flat fee. If einstweilige Verfügung is filed against you: EUR 2,000–8,000+ in court and lawyer fees.
